About

I am very passionate about neuropsychological research. My ambition is to apply (novel) neuroimaging methods to improve diagnostics and develop possible interventions for neurocognitive disorders. My research-oriented academic background spans six years in an international setting, during which I have gained significant experience working with participants and patients. My research interests are diverse, and I have previously worked in research on frontotemporal dementia and cross-cultural psychological phenomena. Currently, I am pursuing a PhD focused on neuroimmunology in the context of Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome . More specifically, I aim to image glial cells and neuroinflammatory mechanisms in the brains of patients with ME/CFS and healthy controls and linking these to peripheral immune responses. I am passionate about helping people affected by invisible disease find a way to be taken seriously and seen. I believe that one of the major strengths of research lies in collaboration and knowledge sharing within the research community and with the general public.
